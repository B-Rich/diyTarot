Features

There is, of course, no substitute for the experience of reading tarot cards with a real deck. However, there are some significant advantages to a digital approach, and I made an effort to leverage them for diyTarot.

No more shuffling.

Okay, that's kind of a joke. But honestly, if you're serious about getting an unbiased batch of cards there's a lot of shuffling involved and tarot cards are usually more awkward to hold than playing cards.

Tarot anywhere you are.

Sometimes I'll be in places where there isn't enough space to lay out cards, or i'll forget to bring a deck with me. As a by-product I can also work on new decks whenever I have a computer handy.

Easy sharing and logging of readings.

Many tarot experts suggest keeping a record of your readings with notes, so you can refer back to them later and see how everything turned out. With diyTarot I can just paste a link in my blog and instantly refer back. Or if I want to share a reading with a friend (I'm particularly fond of sending them horrific ones), it's just a click away!

A tool for learning tarot interactively.

When you're trying to wrap your head around 78 distinct sets of meanings, it can be pretty daunting. And that doesn't even cover reversals! I learned a lot from reading some of the great tarot books and websites out there, but it didn't really start to kick in until I started doing readings every day and designing my own decks.

Create your own decks, and actually use them!

I think every tarot enthusiast eventually thinks about designing their own deck, because the concepts embodied in the cards are quite universal. But most of the time thought is as far as it gets, because even assuming you have the patience and skills to create 78 cards, what would you do afterwards? There are all kinds of wonderfully creative tarot creations on the internet, but most of them are simply for looking at because it's too much of an undertaking to ever make them into a physical deck. No more! Now I can design any deck I like, develop my own meanings for it (or not) and read with it!

A way to keep track of your personal card meanings.

The really experienced card readers always end up developing their own interpretations and personal spins on what the cards mean. Sure, I could write it down in a book or a text file, but why not put them into an online system where I can easily update them anytime, anywhere?
