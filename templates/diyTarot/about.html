What the spork is a diyTarot?

This project was born when I thought of combining my love of tarot cards with my interest in learning Python and web programming. The "diy" (which stands for Do It Yourself) is there because it's exactly that aspect of tarot which fascinates me: the idea of designing your own decks, individual card definitions, and readings.

What the spork?!

To that end, this project is focused around dynamic content: multiple tarot decks, sets of meanings, and card layouts are a key part of the design, and so is the ability to easily modify them later. The central idea is that this is a customized version of the tarot: one's own particular ideas about meanings, reversals, names of suits, and the kinds of images which best sum up each card.

The head, the tail, the whole sporking thing

Want to add tarot readings to your own site, featuring your own design sensibilities? Interested in putting your own decks or spreads up on the web for readings? Maybe you like some features but others drive you up the wall and you'd really prefer to customize? Tired of me ending every sentence with a question mark?

I'm working on streamlining and documenting diyTarot to turn it into an application you can deploy on your own website. Check out Technical Details for the, well, technical details.

Where did all the content come from?

Me, of course! Haha, that's not entirely true. The images for the classic Rider-Waite deck are so old they're public domain at this point. I wrote the descriptions and meanings for all the cards in my own words, but they are a synthesis of all of the booklets, books, and websites on tarot I've read over the years. Whenever I had the choice I favored variety over consistency, which is why some of the cards have one or two where-did-that-come-from predictions.

As far as spreads go, some were made up by me (you can probably guess which ones), but the others are spreads I've seen variations of in a bunch of different places, and some I can't even remember where they came from! (To be honest, memorizing 78 card meanings tends to crowd everything else out.)

The woman behind the curtain

Pay no attention to me! I'm just a programmer in the Valley (by which I mean Silicon, not San Fernando, of course). My background is in programming, but I like to dabble in other fields like usability, graphic design, and drawing stuff. I also have a website, of course.

Portrait of the authoress

My first tarot deck was made with colored pencils on plain paper—it was for Mole Day in Chemistry class and featured pictures of moles instead of people. My favorite deck of all time is the Fantod Pack by Edward Gorey, which is not a real tarot deck but consists of 20 cards that universally predict doom.

In addition to tarot, I am fond of ponies, poodles, poetry, and making jokes about all of the above. And cake! Who doesn't like cake, right?
